Welcome to Maize & Blue Cupboard (MBC)

Your resource for addressing food insecurity within the University of Michigan community.

We offer access to essential food, hygiene products, and educational programs. Here's an overview of the resources available:

  1. Fresh and Shelf-Stable Foods
  2. Personal Hygiene and Kitchen Supplies
  3. Educational Programs
  4. Access by Appointment
  5. Additional Resources and Partnerships

Services Provided

Maize & Blue Cupboard offers a variety of services to support food security:

Fresh and Shelf-Stable Foods

Includes fresh produce, dairy, meats, and a range of pantry items updated regularly.

Personal Hygiene and Kitchen Supplies

Essential hygiene products like soap and toothpaste, as well as kitchen supplies such as cookware and utensils.

Educational Programs

Workshops focused on nutrition, meal planning, budgeting, and food preservation.

Accessing MBC

Here's how you can access the resources at MBC:

Location

MBC is located in the basement of Betsy Barbour Residence Hall, providing convenient access from central campus.

Shopping by Appointment

Appointments can be made online to ensure availability and fair distribution of resources.

Eligibility

All University of Michigan students, faculty, and staff with a valid MCard are eligible for services, regardless of financial need.

Additional Resources

MBC offers more than just food security. It collaborates with various campus and community partners:

Partnerships and Mobile Food Distribution

Collaborations with community organizations to host mobile food pantries and broaden access to resources.

Campus Resource Connections

MBC connects students to important resources like financial aid, mental health services, and wellness programs to support overall well-being.

Volunteer Opportunities

Opportunities for students to engage in community service and gain experience in food management and volunteerism.
